Important Temple Guidelines

🥻 Dress Code (Vastra Paridhaan)

  • **For Men:** You **must remove your shirt/vest** before entering the main **Garbhagriha** (sanctum). This practice signifies **reverence, humility, and purity** when approaching the deity.
  • **For Ladies/Girls:** Traditional Indian attire is **mandatory** (e.g., Saree, Salwar Kameez, Chudidar) to maintain the sanctity of the temple premises.

📵 Electronic & Leather Items

  • **Mobile phones**, cameras, and all electronic devices are strictly prohibited.
  • No leather goods (belts, wallets, bags) or footwear allowed inside the sanctum.

We request your kind cooperation to ensure a serene and spiritual darshan experience.

The Legend and History

The Story of Ghushma

Grishneshwar is revered as the last of the 12 Jyotirlingas. Its name is derived from the legend of the devout woman **Ghushma**. She was known for her unwavering devotion to Lord Shiva, performing the ritual of submerging 101 Shiva Lingas in a pond daily.

Even after a terrible tragedy, her faith remained steadfast. Pleased by her devotion, Lord Shiva appeared and established himself eternally in the form of the Jyotirlinga here, declaring that he would be known by her name: **Ghushmeshwar** (or Grishneshwar).

The temple complex, a masterpiece of ancient architecture, was prominently renovated by Queen Ahilyabai Holkar in the 18th century.
