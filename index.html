The Divine History (‡§á‡§§‡§ø‡§π‡§æ‡§∏)

The **Gh·πõishneshwar Jyotirlinga** is revered as the last of the twelve sacred Jyotirlingas. Its history is deeply intertwined with the legend of the devout couple, Sudharma and his wife, Ghushma. Ghushma's unwavering faith and devotion to Lord Shiva led her to worship 101 Shiva Lingas and immerse them in a tank daily.

After a miraculous event resulting from her devotion, Lord Shiva appeared and established himself here in the form of the Jyotirlinga, naming it after her: **Ghushmeshwar**, which later became **Gh·πõishneshwar**. The present temple structure is known for its exquisite architecture, a testament to the Maratha style, largely rebuilt by **Ahilyabai Holkar** in the 18th century.

Special Instructions

Devotees are generally allowed to enter the Garbhagriha (sanctum sanctorum) to touch the Shivlinga.

  • Dress Code: Traditional Indian attire is encouraged.
  • Photography: Strictly prohibited inside the main temple premises.
  • Peak Days: Timings may be extended or altered during festivals like Maha Shivratri.
